How Big Is Hell?

Belief in the great beyond is quintessentially human. Some version of both heaven and hell have always been staples of culture. Heaven is where you hope your dog and grandma go, hell is where you hope everyone you don't like goes.

By hell, of course, we mean that place of fire, brimstone, and eternal, conscious torment that has become a core Christian doctrine.

But let's be honest: hell is kind of weird, right? Yet even with a strikingly small about of biblical support, the popular notion of a "fiery, eternal conscious torment" persists.

So that got us thinking: what if there were a way to bring more clarity to the idea of hell using modern scientific research techniques? What if we had access to a dataset that could shine more light on the issue? We started asking ourselves one hell of a crazy question… How many people are actually in hell?

We're taking a closer look at the Christian ideas of hell, specifically, who’s there now. If you’re interested in learning along with us, keep scrolling and take this quiz.
